VENTURA

Virtual Decision Room

The Virtual Decision Room (VDR) will enable regional government, regulators and water utility companies to collaboratively plan and evaluate the sustainability of urban growth planning scenarios and its effect on catchment scale water flow and water quality in current, near future and far future scenarios. The integration of systems thinking into the VDR will enable users to reflect on the complexity and dynamics of governance and management structures to support urban water neutrality.

The shared vision for VENTURA in GM developed by the partners is to explore how to be water neutral by 2050 despite population growth, changes in climate and user behaviour. Water neutrality is defined as the avoidance of net gain in water flow and nutrient loads (e.g nitrates and phosphates) reaching water bodies.

Project Partners: Imperial College London, British Geological Survey, Univesity College London, Enfield Council, Environment Agency, Greater Manchester Combined Authority | Funded by EPSRC Grant EP/V042084/1 | Project dates: 01/09/2021 - 30/04/2024

Systems thinking

To achieve water neutrality, collaborative decision-making is required. Our systems thinking perspective explores the complexity and dynamics in governance and organisational decision-making in Manchester and wider environments. Systems thinking insights are visualised in a causal loop diagram, which is a qualitative model of governance factors influencing water neutrality.
