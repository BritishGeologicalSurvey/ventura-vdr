In the first of VDR tool version the model settings will not be editable. Later versions might allow users to change climate change and population change projections.

After creating a custom scenario you will be able to compare near and far future projections to the situation today or the business as usual scenario.

You can explore how water flow, excess water and water quality is expected to change in the near and far future.

Water flow refers to the volume of water that is moving through a particular point or section of a river, stream, or other waterway. It is measured in m3/day.

The term ‘Excess Water’ is currently conceptualised as that part of fallen precipitation which has not infiltrated into groundwater. In this version of WSIMOD, it is calculated as the sum of stormwater discharge to a subcatchment’s outlet and the surface runoff in the same subcatchment. Climate change can increase the amount of excess water as a result of increased rainfall.

Water quality refers to the chemical characteristics of water that determine its suitability for specific uses, such as drinking, swimming, or irrigation. The three indicators for water quality are ammonia, phosphorous and nitrogen.

This VDR tool allows you to view business as usual scenarios and explore how changes to new developments and retrofitting will affect the water network in Manchester and its various catchments.

The VDR is coupled to systems model (WSIMod) that creates simultaneous estimates of the water discharges to sewerage infrastructure and natural watercourses.

This Virtual Decision Room (VDR) tool has been developed through VENTURA, an interdisciplinary team of systems thinking and digital geoscience researchers from University College London (UCL), British Geological Survey (BGS) and Imperial College London (ICL).
